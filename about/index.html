In the 2015 competition season, we competed at the Virginia and Chesapeake Regional. However, we were winners at both competitions, and we moved on to the Championships in St.Louis, Missouri once again. However, we were defeated in our divivion at the championship. In October, we once again hosted the offseason event "Rumble In The Roads." This year the event atracted 27 teams, which meant we had a full competition roster. In December, we participated in Salvation Army bell ringing at Patrick Henry Mall.

During the 2014 season, we competed at the Virginia and Chesapeake Regionals. While we were defeated in the finals at the Virginia Regional, we won the Chesapeake Regional and moved on to play at the championship in St.Louis, Missouri. We played on into the division finals, where we were finally defeated. In the summer, we were one of the teams at NASA’s “Exploration Days” at Busch Gardens, Williamsburg. We also held “Robot Saturdays” where we would demonstrate our robots to visitors at the Virginia Air and Space Center. In October, we hosted the offseason event "Rumble In The Roads", where teams from Virginia and North Carolina came to hone their skills.

In 2013, we built a practice field at the Virginia Air and Space Center for the use of teams around Hampton Roads, we continue to reach out to other teams across Virginia, and we participated in the FIRST® sponsored state wide meeting for team competitiveness. We also reached out to Lee Hall Elementary school, where we started the FIRST® team The Engineers. We ended the season by participating in the Virginia and Palmetto Regionals.

In March of 2012, we were invited to present and demonstrate our robot at a city wide STEM Summit, which focused on the new integrated STEM aspects in the classroom curriculum. Our robot demo was used to highlight the curriculum used in robotic design. we also supported the creation of the FLL team Bricks 'N Bots, ran by a private group. We supported the teams with robot kits, programming and mechanical instructions, and grants for them to travel and compete.

In the 2011 FRC season, we built Genome Gamma to play the game Logomotion. We competed in the Palmetto Regional and won the Engineering Excellence award and the event. We also competed in the Virginia Regional, making it to finals and winning the Industrial design award. Our head coach Matt Wilbur won the Woodie Flowers award, one of the most prestigious awards in FIRST. We made a great showing at the St. Louis World Championship and advanced to the quarter finals.

In 2010, we competed at the Washington DC and Virginia Regionals. We finished the Washington DC Regional with 7 wins and 2 losses, but were not selected to compete in the elimination matches. However, we continued our winning streak by receiving the Underwriters' Laboratory Industrial Safety Award for the second year in a row. At the Virginia Regional, we once again performed well and were finally selected by the number 8 alliance as a competiton for the elimination matches. The alliance was eliminated in the quarter-final matches, but we had broken through and finally made it into the elimination rounds! We capped off the competition by receiving the Motorola Quality Award as the judges recognized the quality build of our robot, Genome Beta.

We made great strides in 2009, competing in two regional competitions: the New Jersey and NASA/VCU Regionals. We finished the New Jersey Regional with 4 wins and 3 losses- 37th place out of 61 teams. At the NASA/VCU Regional, we finished with 5 wins and 2 losses- 15th place out of 60 teams. Unfortunately, no team selected us to partner with them for the elimination matches, and we were poised to go home disappointed. Hard work once again paid off, however, as we won the Underwriters' Laboratory Industiral Safety Award.

In our first year of competing, we won the Rookie Inspiration Award, as the judges recognized our excellent performance in our rookie year.

Our team was founded in 2007 by Matt Wilbur. However, we did not begin competing until 2008.
